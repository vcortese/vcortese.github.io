PERSONAL FINANCE STEPS

Step 1 - Budget

How can we make any improvements if we don't know what is coming in and going out. Create a budget consisting of current income and essential expenses (housing, power, food, transportation). It might help to use tools like YNAB, Mint, or even Excel/Sheets. Once that number is comfortably positive, we can move on.

Did you complete your Budget?

Step 2 - Over the Hump

This is arguably the hardest stage, also the one that will contribute most to significant future growth. First, if your employer offers a retirement match contribute that exact %. Next, we want to eliminate any high interest debt above 7%. That includes ALL credit card debt, which will be easy to maintain since we are cash flow positive. Last is building an emergency fund of 2-4 months expenses within a high interest yielding account. High Yield Savings Accounts, Money Market Accounts, and short term CDs/Treasuries can all work.

Are you over the hump?
